While I’ve been in the Netherlands on an exchange semester I would be lying if I’d say I haven’t gotten homesick. As many other do when moving abroad for the first time. I moved with my family right before I went to the Netherlands. So now when I get homesick I don’t know what home to be sick about. I don’t feel like I live here in the Netherlands. But I have lived here for longer then what is now my home. That makes for a really good opportunity to explore homesickness. Both for me personally and in a broader context.

Down here is an interview with my mom where we talked about homesickness, moving and building a new home.







































































Have you ever gotten homesick?






Yes of course. I remember when I first moved abroad to study. It was really hard at first, but my friend moved with me and we were roommates. So that really helped me feel more like home.
































































After we moved, did you get homesick for our old home?










Yes a little bit. It's always hard to move. But I think the homesickness comes from not being with the people you love.













What has changed after we moved?




To be honest, not much. The biggest change is the new routines that comes with living in a new place. And also now that we live in the center I feel like I see our friends more often.

























Was our old home a big part of your identity?















In some ways. But not in any meaningful ways. Some people from that town really feel strongly about their ties to their home town. But me, not that much.






































Has your identity changed after we moved?






No not really. But now I feel like I meet more of my people and go to more events in the city. Sometimes I felt like that was missing from when we lived in our old home.























Have you gotten the feeling of being at home at our new house?





Yes absolutely. I love living here. But like I said, I think that the feeling of being at home is to be around the people you care about. But of course it took time to get used to living in a new place.


















Was there a specific moment when you felt like you had a new home?





I think it was when we had a proper dinner all together. A couple of days after we moved in. It was just so peaceful after all of the moving.


































What do you miss about our old home?





I really liked how peacful and quiet it was. And also just the familiarity, we lived there for such a long time. But I'm happy we moved. I think we will really make a good home here at our new place.
